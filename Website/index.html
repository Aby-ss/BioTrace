BioTrace

!Image!

BioTrace is an integrated system of camera traps and sound sensors that regularly provides data input to an AI processing hub which can detect animals and record their numbers.

Challenge Information

Find a way to improve the tracking of biodiversity in the wetlands. This involves using technology such as camera traps to identify animals, particularly at night. Propose a small project by determining the appropriate technology to use, where to install it, and how many cameras are needed.

Able to identify animal based on their species.

Able to record and track populations of species in wetlands range over long period of time.

Easily understandable human interface.

Variety of sensors to ensure accurate tracking.

- Active Infrared Sensors

- Passive Infrared Sensors

- Stereo Cameras

- LiDAR Sensors

The inability to track biodiversity poses significant challenges, leading to a loss of essential species knowledge, difficulty in identifying endangered animals, and a lack of control over overpopulated species. Without comprehensive data on various species, understanding their roles within ecosystems becomes limited, hindering informed conservation decisions. Additionally, the absence of accurate monitoring impedes the identification of endangered species, making targeted conservation efforts challenging and potentially exacerbating their decline. Conversely, the unchecked proliferation of certain species due to inadequate tracking can disrupt ecosystems, impacting biodiversity and human activities. Effective biodiversity tracking is crucial for informed conservation, sustainable development, and the preservation of our planet's diverse life forms.
